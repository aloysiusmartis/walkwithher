Real Stories. Real Lives.

Savita's Story
Rescued · Elected Leader
Rescued in 2019 from a brick kiln where she worked 12-hour days with no pay. With aftercare support, Savita found fair-wage work, saved enough to build a house, and at age 20 was elected to her village council, now serving 100 households.
Today: Village council leader fighting for her community
Sabina's Story
Trafficked · Training for Independence
Trafficked at 17 with her younger sister. Lured by a fake job offer, they were sexually exploited. Sabina escaped, then freed her sister with help from a JVI Justice Hub. Two years of aftercare later, she's completing vocational sewing training.
Today: Finishing sewing course, saving to support her family
Yamini's Story
Enslaved · Dreaming Again
Sold into domestic servitude at 17. Worked 6am to 11pm, seven days a week. No wages. Sexually abused. After rescue, JVI secured her back wages and provided trauma counseling. She chose to stay in Delhi and enrolled in tailoring and beautician courses.
Today: Studying to open her own beauty parlor

What Happens in the First 90 Days

Days 1–14 · Safety
A safe place to breathe
Emergency shelter. Medical screening, food, clothing, a quiet room. Many arrive with nothing. This is the first time no one is hurting them.
Days 14–45 · Healing
Beginning to feel human again
Trauma-informed counseling starts. Building trust with people who actually care.
71% of survivors experience depression · 61% suffer from PTSD
Days 45–90 · First Steps
Seeds of a new life
Peer support, life skills, daily choices. The possibility of a different future starts to feel real, and this is when funding often runs out.
